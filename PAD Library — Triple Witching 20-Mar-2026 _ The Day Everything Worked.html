New Rules Documented Today

Each live session generates operational knowledge that isn't found in any book. These are the rules that this day added to the PAD system:

Rule 1 — IV CL as a geopolitical leading indicator

When CL's IV spikes by 5 points or more overnight, look for Middle East geopolitical headlines before trading. Crude oil prices risk before the news breaks. A spike in CL AM's IV is a macro warning sign for all assets.

Rule 2 — In the exact Gamma Flip, price rules

When the price is literally on the Gamma Flip, the CVD becomes unreadable—there are two equal forces colliding, and the algorithms generate maximum noise. Do not read the CVD in that area. Wait for the price to move 8-10 points in either direction before interpreting the CVD.

Rule 3 — Triple Witching can generate two selling waves

The first wave is absorbed—the dealers do their job. The second wave, generated by the end-of-session expiration flow, can overcome institutional absorption. This is why the Triple Witching briefing always says: wide stops, no averaging, normal sizing until the flip breaks with confirmation.

Rule 4 — CVD ES/NQ Divergence predicts the leader

When NQ's CVD is more positive than ES's, NQ will lead the recovery. When NQ's CVD is more negative than ES's, NQ will lead the decline. This divergence was confirmed multiple times during the session—NQ was consistently more extreme in both directions.
