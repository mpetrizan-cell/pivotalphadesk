🔥 MACRO SUMMARY — STAGFLATION SIGNAL CONFIRMED
Today's data package is the most challenging for the Fed: a slowing economy (GDP 2.0% miss) but accelerating inflation (Core PCE Q 4.3%, GDP Price 4.5%) . The labor market is ultra-strong (Claims 189K vs. 215K consensus). Consumer spending is in line. Translation: The Fed can't cut rates—inflation is too hot. It can't raise rates—the economy is slowing. Powell was right to be hawkish yesterday. June cuts are impossible with these numbers. The market opens with last night's positive earnings as support versus this hawkish data as pressure. Maximum tension at the Call Wall 7,150 (GAIA) / 7,200 (Nexa).
🏦 Powell Context — The Institutional Signal the Market Is Processing
Beyond the numbers, the market is digesting Powell's message from yesterday: "Fed independence is at risk." Powell didn't stay on the Board because he likes the job. He stayed because he believes the institution is under threat. With four dissenters (a record since 1992), Warsh's incoming hawkishness , and the Department of Justice investigating—there's an institutional risk premium that the market is beginning to price in. Today's data (stagflation) complicates matters further: the Fed needs independence precisely when it's most difficult to use. Operational implication: greater structural volatility in the coming months.
